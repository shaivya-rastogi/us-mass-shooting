Las Vegas, Parkland, Orlando, Sutherland Springs, San Bernardino. Fort Hood, Virginia Tech, Red Lake. Luby’s, Atlanta, Columbine. It is alarming that reading these lists, members of at least one decade will associate the name with the horror and loss of a mass shooting. These are just a few of the many incidents that have plagued the history of the United States over the last 60 years.



The fatalities and injuries have only increased over the years with an alarming spike after 2014. Back in 2015, journalists at the New York Times reported that on average, shootings that left more than 4 or more people wounded or dead occurred more than once a day, according to compilations of episodes derived from news reports occurred more than once a day. This is a terrifying statistic to think about.

United States is experiencing a gun epidemic across the country. With incidents spanning over the past 6 decades, we can see the state-wise distribution of incidents, fatalities and total victim count. Despite having the most number of shootings, California is second to Nevada in victim count. This can be attributed to the Las Vegas shooting in 2017. Clearly Texas, California and Nevada stand out overall.

The US makes up less than 5% of the world’s population, but holds 31% of global mass shooters.

The Parkland shooting in Florida, ignited rapid discussions, multiple global protests and activist movements to encourage a dialogue about the increase in gun violence, possible gun law reforms and the safety of students. While school shootings seem to be at a peak, this decade has in fact seen a larger number of victims in open public areas with 842 victims. The 2000s had almost 179 victims at school shootings. The race of the perpetrators in mass shootings is cause for widespread media coverage. Throughout the last six decades, we observed racial diversity in the perpetrators, however, through our visual exploration of the data set it is clear that White perpetrators are predominant among all other races.

Gun control has been a topic of debate for a long time. Political leaders in the United States over the past 60 decades have introduced various gun laws. While the heartless assassinations of President John F. Kennedy(1963), Attorney General Robert Kennedy(1968) and Martin Luther King Jr.(1968) led to the introduction of the Gun Control Act in 1968 by President Lyndon B. Johnson, the attempted assassination of President Ronald Reagan led to the introduction of Brady Handgun Violence Protection Act - which was signed into law by President Bill Clinton. Massacres have also resulted in movements across the US to curb gun violence, the most recent incident being shootings in Parkland, Florida. The national uproar resulted in passing of the STOP School Violence Act on March 23, 2018, which increased funding for metal detectors, security training, and similar safety measures.






With the unpredictability of mass shooting incidents understanding the history of it and how political, legal and social events impact it is of utmost importance. Hopefully, seeing the numbers laid out helps us fathom the extent to which these horrific incidents are impacting human survival and allow us to continue the current dialogue of what can be done to bring down the spike of these atrocities.
